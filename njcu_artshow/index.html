Our goal as artists to acheive for the Third Annual LATI Colloquium - Spring 2020

...with a Social Consciousness to address the current issues in Latin America and the Caribbeans due to the stronghold of US Hegemonic Domination!

Despite the myth of human progress under the “Free Market” Capitalist system, it is clear now that this same system is destroying not only the livelihood of many people in Latin American countries but the world as well. I’m reminded of a quote by Ernest Fischer who said the following regarding the arts:

“In a decaying society, art, if it is truthful, must also reflect decay. And unless it wants to break faith with its social function, art must show the world as changeable. And help to change it.”
― Ernst Fischer

That is why we are reaching out to all progressive and social consciousness artists that can express their concerns through their artworks.

Overview
By putting the spotlight on artists who through their art and activism raise awareness on issues in the Caribbean and Latin America Countries due to US Stronghold Hegemony Ideology. This exhibition at NJCU will illuminate and celebrate the power of narrative art as a tool in the toolbox for challenging the Capitalist “Free Market” system. The exhibition itself will be populated with artworks that carry a compelling narrative, and the event will celebrate the artists for engaging towards an open dialogue discussion through their art and activism.
Artist Criteria for Submission:
Submitting artists must:
  • be over 18-years of age
  • be a current student at NJCU
  • have as the content and purpose of their art challenging Capitalism and/or raising awareness around issues in Latin America and the Caribbean
Submission Process/Artwork Restrictions:
Due to the amount of space we have at the art gallery in the Gilligan Student Union Building, you can only submit one or two actual works you hope to have selected for the exhibition. Please send digital jpegs files along with the names and dimensions for us to review it. Email them to the following emails: csanchez11@njcu.edu or atoledo@njcu.edu
Because of the constraints of the exhibition space in which the show will take place, submissions must meet the following restrictions:
  • Artwork (including dimension of frame) must be between 20” and 30” in width and height, and should not exceed the height of 40' and width 30”
  • Hanging the art requires a special wiring system; frames or support structures for the art must be able to accommodate special hardware screwed along the backside top by the exhibition’s installation team
  • Works must be two-dimensional or three-dimensional work.
  • Only individual artists (not collaborative projects) may apply.
Note: As we approach near the deadline for artists to submit their works we might have to make adjustments to accommodate other artists. That is why all artist needs to submit all digital photo files, dimensions, and titles for review before the deadline Jan.25, 2020 so we can make proper arrangements
